## Scene

An AI meeting recap arrives after a call. It is neat, fast, and attractive, but
the summary is not the meeting. It may omit context, flatten disagreement, or
assign ownership too cleanly.

## Where And When

Ordinary office meeting or remote-work setting.

## Who Is Present

Meeting participants and whoever later relies on the recap.

## What The Protagonist Wants

The reader wants the meeting to become action without having to write or argue
over notes.

## What Is Being Looked At, Changed, Asked, Or Discovered

AI-generated recap, minutes, highlights, or action items.

## Friction

The recap is tidy, but participants may still need to edit, delete, and argue
with it because the summary has lost context.

## Downside If Ignored

The wrong person owns the wrong work, or a disagreement disappears from the
record.

## Concrete Details

- object: recap document
- phrase: action item
- number: not needed
- before/after: messy meeting becomes neat recap; recap requires correction
- human reaction: people edit/delete or contest the summary

## Exact Source Evidence

`reader-world-research-packet.md` records Microsoft meeting-recap research and

## Cold Reader Test

A cold reader can picture leaving a call, opening the recap, and finding a neat
version of events that does not quite match what happened.

## Thesis Relevance

Shows why AI polish needs enough underlying context to be corrected.
