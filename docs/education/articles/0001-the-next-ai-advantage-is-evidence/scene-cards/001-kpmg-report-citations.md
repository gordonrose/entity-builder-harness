# Scene Card

## Metadata

- Scene title: KPMG Report Citations
- Article candidate: The Answer Has To Stand Up
- Source packet: `../source-packet.md`
- Source evidence: `../reader-world-research-packet.md`
- Status: usable

## Scene

KPMG publishes a report called *Redefining excellence in the age of agentic
AI*. The report looks serious and authoritative. Public reporting then says
the case studies and citations do not hold up.

## Where And When

Public business/technology discourse around AI reports and AI credibility.
Avoid exact calendar-date framing in the article body.

## Who Is Present

KPMG, report readers, cited organizations, downstream commentators, and anyone
who relies on the report because it looks official.

## What The Protagonist Wants

The reader wants to trust polished, authoritative work without having to
verify every citation personally.

## What Is Being Looked At, Changed, Asked, Or Discovered

A polished report with citations, case studies, and institutional authority.
The original report no longer appears publicly available from KPMG, so the
article should link the Financial Times and TechRadar reporting and say that
plainly.

## Friction

The form says "serious work." The evidence underneath does not.

## Downside If Ignored

False claims travel because the report looks credible.

## Concrete Details

- object: AI report
- phrase: citations and case studies
- number: 45 citations, with only five reported as accurately pointing to
  sources
- before/after: report looks authoritative; evidence collapses under review
- human reaction: embarrassment and loss of trust

## Exact Source Evidence

`reader-world-research-packet.md` records the Financial Times and TechRadar
sources. The packet also records that the original KPMG report was not found
as a direct public KPMG link during research.

## Cold Reader Test

A cold reader can picture a polished report being trusted because it looks
official, then challenged because the citations do not hold up.

## Thesis Relevance

Shows the article's thesis in public: AI-shaped work can acquire institutional
confidence before it has earned it.

## Opening Strength

- Strong enough to open: yes
- Why: public, concrete, evidence-shaped, and fresh for this article
- What additional material would make it stronger: a direct KPMG report link
  if it reappears before publication; otherwise keep the article explicit that
  the report is being referenced through public reporting
