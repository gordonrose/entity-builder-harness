## Source Scene Used

The article opens with the KPMG AI report citation failure because it is a
fresh, public, evidence-shaped scene that does not reuse the author's prior
stakeholder-trust anecdote.

The original KPMG report no longer appears publicly available from KPMG. The
article therefore names the report but routes readers through the Financial
Times and TechRadar reporting, and says that plainly in the opening.

## Selected Thesis Followed

The answer has to stand up: AI-shaped work should carry enough evidence that
it can be explained, corrected, defended, and improved after it leaves the
original conversation.

## Reader-World Examples Used And Avoided

Used:

- KPMG report citation failure
- Air Canada chatbot bereavement-fare dispute
- AI meeting recap research
- Mata v. Avianca fake legal citations

Avoided:

- The customer worth 20% of revenue and 30 promised-but-unbuilt features,
  because it was reused from a prior article and is now retired as a major
  example.

## Artifact Availability Notes

- KPMG report: no direct public KPMG link found during research. The article
  says the report no longer appears publicly available from KPMG and links the
  Financial Times and TechRadar reporting.
- GPTZero analysis: used through TechRadar's summary unless a primary GPTZero
  artifact is found before publication.

## Terms Translated Or Delayed

- `Architecture decision record` appears only after the reader understands
  durable explanations.
- `Harness` is mostly translated as "rules around my own AI assistant."
- `Token use` appears in the metrics section as a practical signal of repeated
  AI work.

## What Works

- The first third now sets the reader-world table before the thesis.
- The article avoids the previous abstract jump from one anecdote to memory.
- The KPMG opening is fresh, current, public, and directly about evidence.
- The opening now explains why the original KPMG report is not directly
  linked.
- The harness story now reads as the author's miniature version of a public
  pattern.

## What May Still Need Revision

- The second act would be stronger with a before/after thin-log excerpt.
- The title is better than the old one but still needs taste-testing.
- The ending lands cleanly but could become more memorable.
