# v0.1 Candidates Prompt

Using the extracted material below, generate candidate content ideas.

Create:

- 10 candidate short public articles, or fewer if the material is thin
- 5 candidate 30-minute talks
- 5 candidate 30-minute lesson plans with 50-minute extension paths
- 10 teaching assets
- 5 possible recurring series ideas

For each idea, explain:

- premise
- tension chain
- lesson
- story arc
- source scene inventory
- source-packet viability
- reader-world research viability
- real reader-world bridge
- 2 to 3 fresh reader-world examples that could set the table before the
  thesis
- example-ledger reuse risk
- likely scene cards
- missing material
- why this is the strongest available story, not merely a true lesson
- what stronger or weaker thesis it is competing against
- audience
- comedic angle
- risk
- evidence from the logs
- why this is worth making
- whether the candidate should advance, gather reader-world research, scan a
  wider window, or be rejected

For lesson-plan candidates, also include:

- 30-minute learning promise
- 50-minute extension
- worked example
- participant activity
- debrief question

Do not write full drafts yet.

Prioritize ideas that are specific, surprising, useful, and human. Remove any
idea that could have been generated without reading the logs.

Before finalizing the candidate list, compare the strongest candidates against
each other. Reject true-but-weak lessons if the source material contains a
larger, more concrete, more surprising, or more audience-relevant arc.

Do not treat "hook" as a punchy line. Use a tension chain: concrete moment,
visible pressure, reader question, deeper problem, complication, and earned
landing.

For public article candidates, do not assume one strong author-owned anecdote
is enough. Identify the external or author-owned reader-world examples needed
to set the scene before the article states its thesis.

Prefer `no publishable article this cycle` over a weak public article.
