# Storytelling Profile

## Prefer

- A specific moment first
- The problem I thought I was solving
- The problem I was actually solving
- Tension before lesson
- Concrete evidence before abstraction
- A clean landing rather than a grand universal claim

## Avoid

- Mechanical commit summaries
- Ten loosely related ideas in one piece
- Generic "what I learned today" structure
- Abstract lessons without a scene, decision, mistake, or surprise

## Current Calibration Notes

- Look for the hidden story inside the day's work, not just the work itself.
- A good talk or post should make the author think: "I did not realize that was
  the interesting part."
- For public AI material, the story should move from a concrete repo incident
  to a wider orientation frame. The critique should arrive as discovery or
  upgrade, not exposure.
