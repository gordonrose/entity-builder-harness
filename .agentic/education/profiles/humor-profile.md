# Humor Profile

## Prefer

- Dry understatement
- Recognition humor from real technical friction
- Irony where the computer is technically correct
- Small absurdities in process, naming, tools, or requirements
- Humor that arrives after a concrete incident

## Avoid

- Forced punchlines
- Fake standup rhythm
- Smugness
- Whimsy not grounded in the work
- Jokes that could have been written without reading the logs

## Current Calibration Notes

- Humor should support teaching and recognition.
- If a joke makes the author sound less credible or less kind, cut it.
- For hype-adjacent audiences, let humor include the reader rather than catch
  them out. The joke should point at the situation, not at the reader's
  insecurity.
- Keep the dry edge, but make the surface hospitable.
