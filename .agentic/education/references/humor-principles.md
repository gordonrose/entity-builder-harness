# Humor Principles

Use these as durable quality principles, not as source imitation.

- Prefer recognition humor over detachable jokes.
- Understatement often fits technical teaching better than punchlines.
- Humor should come from real tension, contradiction, or absurdity.
- Avoid humor that punches down at students, users, or collaborators.
- Cut jokes that weaken clarity or trust.
