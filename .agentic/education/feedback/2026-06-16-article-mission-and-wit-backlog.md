## Remaining Harness Gap

The education harness still does not explicitly ask what the article is trying
to do to the reader as an experience.

It also does not deliberately search for humor, wit, or authorial bite once
the story is structurally sound. This leaves obvious opportunities unused.

## Article Mission Backlog

Add a mission layer before drafting or during editor commissioning.

The mission should answer:

- What should this article do to the reader?
- What should the reader feel sharper about after reading?
- What self-image should the article help them inhabit?
- What anxiety, vanity, confusion, or unspoken pressure is the piece working
  with?
- What should the article not do: scold, flatter, over-explain, dunk, or hide
  the lesson?

This is different from thesis. Thesis says what the article argues. Mission
says what effect the article should have on a real reader.

## Humor And Wit Backlog

Add a humor/wit pass after the source, story, and structure are working.

The pass should look for:

- dry situational wit already present in the material
- small human observations that make the prose feel lived
- pressure-release lines after dense or serious passages
- authorial bite that clarifies the truth without turning mean
- callbacks that earn a smile because the reader now understands the situation

Avoid:

- pasted-on jokes
- gag-heavy paragraphs
- smugness toward the audience
- making the piece less trustworthy by treating serious source material too
  lightly
- humor that arrives before the reader understands the stakes

## Specific Instruction To Preserve

Humor should come from accurate seeing. It should feel like the author noticed
the absurdity inside the situation, not like a separate joke layer was bolted
on after the fact.
