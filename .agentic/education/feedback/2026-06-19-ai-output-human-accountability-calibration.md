# AI Output Human Accountability Calibration

## Context

During the `AI Work Needs A Workbench` article cycle, generated drafts moved
toward polished magazine-essay prose and engineering-workflow explanation. The
user's final preferred version moved closer to public teaching: clearer,
slower, more explicit about reader prerequisites, and centered on the balance
between AI speed and human accountability.

## Core Correction

The strongest thesis is not simply that AI chats need Git-style workbenches.
The stronger thesis is:

> AI output now moves faster than human accountability. The harness exists to
> preserve speed while restoring enough control, inspectability, and ownership
> for a human to responsibly use the output.

## What The Generated Drafts Overdid

- Over-optimized for magazine compression before public teaching clarity.
- Treated the workbench as the article's topic instead of as the mechanism
  that balances speed with accountability.
- Assumed too much reader familiarity with engineering convergence, `main`,
  branches, pull requests, and preprints.
- Jumped from human-team workflow to AI-chat workflow without explaining what
  does not transfer: chats are not naturally separate accountable individuals.
- Used some crafted wit where plainer reader-orienting explanation was needed.

## What The User-Final Direction Restored

- Clear public teaching first, authorial polish second.
- Reader desire first: AI speed is genuinely valuable.
- Downside second: speed creates inspection, testing, explanation, and
  ownership pressure.
- Workbench as counterweight, not slogan.
- Chat individuality as manufactured upstream by the harness through branch,
  worktree, session log, write guards, and convergence rules.
- Accessible examples and explanations before internal harness vocabulary.

## Lines To Preserve As Calibration

- "I really like how quickly AI works. But we shouldn't think that speed
  doesn't come with a lot of downsides."
- "Work can now be created faster than it can be inspected, understood,
  tested, explained, and owned."
- "If AI gives me ten times the output but only one tenth of the
  inspectability, I have not become ten times faster. I have become the owner
  of a faster mess."
- "The assistant is not accountable. I am."

## Durable Profile Updates

- Audience: assume competent, AI-curious readers who may not know engineering
  convergence. Build prerequisite ladders before analogies.
- Voice: add a public technical explainer mode: direct, patient, slightly
  conversational, and willing to repeat the key point plainly.
- Humor: prefer accessible recognition over crafted literary wit.
- Storytelling: require analogy transfer tests. Explain what does and does not
  transfer before using a technical analogy.
- Structure: add a public technical explainer shape: reader desire, hidden
  cost, public examples, author-owned version, plain model, harness mechanism,
  practical payoff.

## Harness Process Updates

- Article briefs need reader prerequisite ladders and analogy transfer tests.
- Opening labs need a plain teaching opening candidate.
- Writer guidance needs a public technical explainer mode.
- Calibration workflow should support AI-draft versus user-final comparison.
- Article cycles should be able to produce a calibration report so reusable
  taste lessons do not remain trapped in one draft.
