# Scene Card

Use one scene card per possible article scene.

## Metadata

- Scene title:
- Article candidate:
- Source packet:
- Source evidence:
- Status: usable / needs reporting / rejected

## Scene

Describe the scene in ordinary language.

## Where And When

Use enough context for the reader to picture the moment. Avoid calendar dates
unless the exact date matters to the reader-facing stakes.

## Who Is Present

Name the human actor or point of view.

If the main actor is `system`, `record`, `process`, `AI work`, or `decision`,
this is not yet a scene.

## What The Protagonist Wants

State the practical goal.

## What Is Being Looked At, Changed, Asked, Or Discovered

Name the concrete artifact or action.

## Friction

What makes the moment difficult, awkward, surprising, or revealing?

## Downside If Ignored

What could go wrong?

## Concrete Details

List imageable details:

- object:
- phrase:
- number:
- before/after:
- human reaction:

## Exact Source Evidence

Link or quote short snippets from source material. Keep quoted material short.

## Cold Reader Test

Could a cold reader picture this without repo vocabulary?

What would they retell?

## Thesis Relevance

How does this scene support or complicate the article's thesis?

- Strong enough to open: yes / no
- Why:
- What additional material would make it stronger:
