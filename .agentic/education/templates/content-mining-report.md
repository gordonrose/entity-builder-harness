Content types may include 3-minute blog post, 30-minute talk, lecture anecdote,
classroom exercise, debugging story, design decision story,
research/process reflection, comedy bit, warning label, or metaphor.

## Candidate 3-Minute Blog Posts

For each candidate include:

- Title
- One-sentence premise
- Why this works
- Hidden lesson
- Opening hook
- Suggested 5-part structure
- Best audience
- Comedic angle
- Risk
- Scores for educational value, entertainment value, originality, voice fit,
  and ease of writing
- Source material IDs

## Candidate 30-Minute Talks

For each candidate include:

- Title
- Subtitle
- Core promise
- Why this talk exists
- Best audience
- Central story
- Technical spine
- Emotional spine
- Comedic spine
- Timed structure
- Audience interaction
- Possible slides
- Best story moments
- Risk
- Scores for teaching value, story strength, humor potential, originality,
  lecturer fit, and ease of preparation
- Source material IDs

## Teaching Assets

Include classroom examples, exercises, discussion prompts, debugging exercises,
spot-the-mistake examples, mini case studies, analogies, diagrams, and slide
moments.

For each asset include:

- what it teaches
- how to use it
- student level
- source material IDs

## Voice Calibration

Identify:

- phrases or rhythms that fit the author
- humor that fits
- humor that would feel fake
- natural technical depth
- what to preserve
- what to avoid
- concise reusable voice update

## Best Picks

Recommend:

- strongest 3-minute blog post
- strongest 30-minute talk
- strongest teaching asset
- funniest usable moment
- most intellectually serious moment
- most relatable beginner-facing moment

## Iteration Questions

Ask up to five high-value questions about tone, audience, humor, technical
depth, preferred formats, and what felt unlike the author.

## Specificity Audit

For each strong candidate, answer:

- Does this clearly come from the logs?
- Is there a concrete incident?
- Is there a real decision, mistake, surprise, or tradeoff?
- Could this have been generated without reading the logs?

Remove or rewrite ideas that fail this audit.

## Machine-Smell Audit

Remove or rewrite generic phrasing, including:

- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "It is important to note"
- "This highlights the importance of"
- "A key takeaway is"
- "By leveraging"
- "Robust and scalable"
- "Seamless"
- "Journey"
- "Delve"
- "Unlock"
- "Game-changer"
