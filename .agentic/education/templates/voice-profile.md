# Voice Profile

## Current Goal

Build a public teaching voice as a university lecturer: clear, thoughtful,
technically credible, funny without trying too hard, and honest about the
messiness of real work.

## Tone

- Reflective
- Direct
- Curious
- Slightly dry humor
- Comfortable admitting confusion
- Interested in process, not just results
- Practical rather than performative

## Avoid

- Corporate thought-leadership tone
- Overly polished motivational writing
- Generic lessons learned
- Fake jokes
- Clickbait
- Grand claims unsupported by actual work
- Productivity-influencer cadence

## Prefer

- Specific incidents
- Concrete technical details
- Honest uncertainty
- Lessons grounded in practice
- Humor from recognition rather than punchlines
- Teaching through stories

## Useful Recurring Moves

- The problem I thought I had was X. The problem I actually had was Y.
- This looked like a technical problem. It was really a naming, design, or
  communication problem.
- Future me had left present me a trap.
- The computer was technically correct, which is the most annoying kind of
  correct.
- This is the kind of thing that seems obvious immediately after it has cost an
  afternoon.
